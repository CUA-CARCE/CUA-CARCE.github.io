About CARCE

Our Mission & Vision

Founded in 2010, CARCE has grown to become one of the world's foremost centers for advanced computer engineering research — uniting fundamental science with transformative technology.

Our Mission

Advancing the Frontier
of Computing

CARCE exists to push the boundaries of what computing can achieve — through rigorous fundamental research, bold applied engineering, and a steadfast commitment to the public good. We believe that the most important problems in computer engineering require a long-term, multidisciplinary approach that transcends traditional academic silos.

Our eight institutes and 24 laboratories span the full spectrum of computer engineering — from quantum hardware to climate-aware software systems — united by shared infrastructure, collaborative culture, and a vision of technology in service of humanity.

Our Values

The Principles We Live By

I.
Scientific Rigor
Every claim is subjected to the highest standards of empirical and theoretical scrutiny. We prioritize truth over novelty.
II.
Long-Horizon Thinking
We invest in research that may take decades to bear fruit, trusting that fundamental advances have the greatest eventual impact.
III.
Open Science
Findings, code, and data are made freely available wherever possible. Knowledge advances fastest when freely shared.
IV.
Human Benefit
Every research programme is evaluated against a simple question: does this make things better for people and for our planet?
V.
Diversity & Inclusion
The best science emerges from the broadest possible range of perspectives, backgrounds, and lived experiences.
VI.
Collaborative Excellence
Breakthroughs emerge at the intersection of disciplines. We cultivate deep cross-institute and international collaboration.

History

CARCE Timeline

2010
CARCE Founded
Center established with founding gift of $40M and three inaugural institutes: AI, Systems Architecture, and Formal Methods.
2013
Quantum Computing Institute Launched
Institute 02 established following landmark NSF quantum information grant. First qubit coherence experiments begin.
2016
Biomedical & Robotics Institutes Opened
Expansion into health computing and autonomous systems, funded by NIH and DARPA partnerships.
2019
New Cambridge Campus
Move to purpose-built 180,000 sq ft research facility featuring quantum cleanrooms, robotics testbeds, and immersive XR studios.
2022
Climate Institute & $100M Endowment
Institute 08 launched to address sustainable computing. CARCE endowment reaches $100M. 1,000th publication milestone.
2025
CERN Partnership & Quantum Milestone
Historic 1,000-qubit coherence achievement. Partnership with CERN announced. 24 laboratories now active across 8 institutes.
