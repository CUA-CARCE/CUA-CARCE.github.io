🌿  Climate Computing

The Institute for Climate & Sustainable Computing addresses the dual challenge of greening computation and leveraging computation to address climate change. Three labs tackle energy efficiency, climate modeling, and policy.

Laboratories

Three Specialized Laboratories

Each laboratory operates as an independent research unit with dedicated faculty, postdocs, and PhD students, sharing Institute-wide infrastructure.

⚡
GCA

Green Computer Architecture Laboratory

Lead: Dr. Wei Li

Energy-proportional computing, approximate computing, near-threshold voltage operation, and architectural techniques for dramatic reductions in computing energy consumption.

Carbon-Aware SchedulingApproximate ML InferenceNear-Threshold ProcessorsEnergy Harvesting Systems

AICSL

AI for Climate Science Laboratory

Lead: Dr. Nana Osei

Machine learning for climate simulation acceleration, downscaling, extreme weather prediction, and carbon cycle modeling. Physics-informed neural networks for earth systems.

Sustainable Data Center & Systems Lab

Renewable energy integration, liquid cooling, workload migration, and lifecycle analysis for hyperscale and edge data centers seeking net-zero operation.
